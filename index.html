What Park+ does well

🏗️
Massive surface area, executed cleanly

Park+ has pulled off something genuinely hard — FASTag, e-challans, insurance, loans, parking, and ANPR hardware, all under one roof. Each feature works. The FASTag recharge flow is fast, the challan check is accurate, and the B2B side (boom barriers, ANPR) is a category in itself. That breadth is a moat.

🔗
Smart ecosystem play

Embedding inside PhonePe (200M+ users) rather than just building standalone shows sharp product-market thinking. Park+ is positioning itself as infrastructure for vehicle owners — not just another app. The Sequoia/Matrix backing validates this at scale.

📊
Data flywheel is real

100M+ FASTag transactions, 20M+ listed cars. That's a dataset that can power personalized offers, insurance pricing, and EV charging prediction. The data exists — the question is whether the product surface reflects it to users.

02

The friction I found

Core Problem Statement

New users open the app and don't know what it's for — them.

I opened Park+ as a fresh user and felt immediately overwhelmed. FASTag recharge. E-challan. Car insurance. Test drives. Personal loans. New cars. There's no entry point that says "here's what matters to you." Every feature competes for attention on the home screen simultaneously.

This isn't a visual design problem. It's an intent problem. The app knows a lot about cars — but it doesn't know anything about this user yet. So it shows everything, hoping something sticks.

Cognitive overload
Low activation
Feature blindness
No personalization hook
📉
Why this hurts the business

A user who doesn't understand the app in the first 30 seconds is very likely to drop off. With 50M+ downloads, even a 5% improvement in day-1 activation rate is a significant number. More importantly — if Park+ doesn't know user intent at signup, it can't personalize push notifications, surface the right upsell, or route B2B vs B2C users correctly.

04

Product Requirements Document

PRD: Personalised Onboarding Intent Flow

Feature Proposal · Park+ Consumer App · v1.0

Author: Vivek (PM Intern Candidate)
Status: Proposal
Priority: High
Platform: Android & iOS
Date: June 2026
1. Problem Statement

Park+ offers 6+ distinct use cases on its home screen (FASTag, challan, insurance, parking, car discovery, loans). A new user arriving for the first time has no clear entry point and no way to tell the app what they need. This leads to cognitive overload, low feature activation, and poor Day-1 retention.

The app currently treats every user identically at first launch. In a product where the FASTag user and the new-car-buyer have almost zero overlap in needs, this is a missed personalization opportunity at the most valuable moment — first impression.

3. User Stories
"As a new Park+ user who primarily needs FASTag, I want the app to understand this upfront so I don't have to hunt for the recharge button buried under car loan offers."
"As a user who just got a challan, I want to open the app and immediately see how to check and pay it — not scroll past parking and test drive banners."
"As a Park+ product team member, I want intent data at D0 so we can run better-segmented push campaigns and reduce uninstalls from irrelevant notifications."
4. Acceptance Criteria
  • Intent screen is shown exactly once — on first app launch after install. Never shown again to returning users.
  • Screen displays 5 intent options: FASTag & Tolls, Challan Check & Payment, Car Insurance, Find Parking, Explore New Cars.
  • User can select one primary intent and proceed. Multi-select is a v2 consideration.
  • A "Skip / Explore everything" option is available (tapped users get default home screen, intent recorded as null).
  • Home screen tile order and default tab reorder based on selected intent within the same session.
  • Intent preference is stored in user profile — editable later under Settings → Personalize My Park+.
  • All other features remain accessible — intent only affects ranking, not visibility.
  • Intent selection event is logged to analytics with timestamp, device type, and selected option.
5. Out of Scope (v1)

Multi-intent selection, ML-based intent inference from behavior, B2B/enterprise user differentiation, intent-based notification cadence changes (v2), and A/B testing framework setup (assumed existing).

6. Risks & Mitigations
⚠ Risk

Users select an intent that doesn't match their actual recurring behavior, leading to a mis-personalized experience after day 1.

✓ Mitigation

Allow intent reset under Settings. Also, monitor behavioral signals (what user actually taps) and allow home screen to adapt over time — intent is a starting point, not a cage.

⚠ Risk

High skip rate — users dismiss the screen without selecting, reducing the data value of the feature.

✓ Mitigation

Frame skip CTA as "I'll explore on my own" not just "Skip" — implies agency. Test copy variants. Track skip rate in analytics as a health metric.

7. Rollout Timeline
Week 1–2
Discovery & Wireframes

Finalize intent options with data team (what are the top 5 first actions of new users today?). Design wireframes. Align with eng on home screen reordering logic.

Week 3–4
Build & Internal Testing

Engineering builds intent screen + reordering logic. QA on both platforms. Verify analytics events fire correctly.

Week 5
A/B Launch (10% of new installs)

Run test group vs control. Monitor D1 activation, skip rate, intent completion. No full rollout until metrics validated.

Week 6–7
Full Rollout + Iteration

Roll out to 100% new installs if A/B shows positive signal. Document learnings for v2 (multi-intent, ML inference).

One growth idea

Bonus Proposal

Park+ EV Readiness Score

Park+ already has FASTag data, location data, and is deploying EV charging infra. The next move: give every user a personalized "EV Readiness Score" based on their driving patterns. Show them how much they'd save switching to EV, which charging stations are near their regular routes, and partner with EV brands (Tata, MG, Ola Electric) for targeted test drives. Turns data Park+ already has into a new acquisition channel for both users and B2B clients.
